Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Rhetoric plays a role in design because we are persuading viewers to look at our pieces through good, compelling design. Technology plays a role because we are learning to use technology to our advantage. Ethics plays a role in design because we are learning how to make accessable and inclusive design. We considered the use of rhetoric when we did the UX design study and asked participants visceral, behavioral,a nd reflective questions. We considered technology when we used templates to create logos. We considered ethics when we coded included descriptions of our images in our code for visually impaired people.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

We are learning about space, alignment, balance, contrast, proximity and contrast and how to use them. We do this through our readings, five minute free writes, and group discussions.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In this course, we often share our designs with each other. We learn to give and recieve constructive criticism, while applying the design principles. We do this when we come to class prepared with a project, show our groupmates, and give and recieve feedback.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

After workshops and design studies, we are assigned to revise our work. Not only listening but being able to apply criticism, is an important part of design. I am currently doing this as I fix my portfolio based off my professor's feedback. We also did this in our UX design study when we revised after feedback from participants.

5. Learn to present your work.

May it be to the entire class or in groups, we present often in class. Becoming comfortable with this is something that takes practice. I learned to present my work when I presented my UX design project to a small group. I also learn this during workshops with my group members.
